Behavioral Anomalies:
They aren't mimicking the wind anymore.

When the flock lands in the canopy, the trees go completely silent—no crickets, no frogs, nothing. They just sit there, hundreds of them, looking down at the tent. Last night, one of them cleared its throat and whispered my childhood nickname. Not in a bird voice. In my father's exact voice.

They’re learning the names of everyone who enters these woods.
